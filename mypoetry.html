My Poetry

To the one that got away

To that piece of plantain that fell face flat
En route between the frying pan
And flat plate, laced with a kitchen paper towel
To soak up the oil,
I will need a full roll of kitchen paper towels to soak up
My tears shed over the fact that you fell, splat!


I would have picked you up at once had I noticed,
But I was self-absorbed thinking about eating,
And so absorbed with flipping the other plantain pieces
That I let you fall between the cracks of my vision,
And for that I am sorry.
And I’m sorry I let the devil get to you
After the count of three.


Four and a quarter years later,
Do you know, I still lay awake at night thinking about you?
You were the one that got away,
The plantain piece that took my breath away,
From all that frying.
But I’d be lying if I said I haven’t moved on;
There’s a whole bunch of plantains in the fridge
Right now as we speak.
And, over the years,
I have worked my way through enough bunches
To mend my broken stomach.



2021

For Temi

Things will be better next year,
I swear.
The weather will be nice,
we’ll run around like mice;
and we’ll be okay.
Maybe not today,
or the next day.
Or the day after that…
Bomboclaat!


But someday…
somehow,
someway,
hopefully by 2021,
this pandemic shit will be done.



Rock-a-bye-baby

Memories form while
we’re not watching.
She’s watching me,
I’m watching the baby.
Rock-a-bye baby,
Rock-a-bye bye.
You laid in my arms still,
you lay in hers still.
